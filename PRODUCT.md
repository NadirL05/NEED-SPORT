# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Football supporters aged 16–40, primarily French but also European. They follow Ligue 1, Premier League, LaLiga, Serie A and major national teams. They want authentic official jerseys — not replicas — and they want them fast. They care about the real article: the fabric, the badge, the exact cut worn on the pitch.

## Product Purpose

NEED SPORT is a premium French jersey e-commerce brand founded in Paris in 2024. It sells only official licensed football jerseys from clubs and national teams worldwide. The promise: authentic kits, certified fabrics, 48h delivery across metropolitan France.

Success looks like: a supporter who was going to settle for a replica lands on NEED SPORT, sees it differently, and converts on the real thing.

## Brand Personality

Athletic · Editorial · Precise

Voice is confident, terse, and authoritative — like a football broadcast, not a lifestyle blog. Copy is short. Headers are statements, not questions. The brand does not explain itself.

Emotional goal: the feeling you get when you hold the real shirt for the first time and know it's the exact one the players wore.

## Anti-references

- Generic sports big-box retailers (Decathlon, Sports Direct visual language — loud, discount-coded)
- Replica / counterfeit sites — cheap gradient backgrounds, aggressive popups
- Overly playful streetwear aesthetics (Supreme, Palace) — that's a different tribe
- Template-looking Shopify stores with cookie-cutter card grids and no visual identity

## Design Principles

1. **Authenticity above decoration** — every visual choice should feel like it belongs on a pitch, not in a marketing deck
2. **Scarcity signals premium** — white space is not empty; it is confidence
3. **The product is the hero** — imagery and photography carry the page; UI steps back
4. **Speed as respect** — fast load, fast checkout, no friction
5. **Editorial tension** — large type against restraint; contrast between scale and quiet

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. The site serves fans on mobile at match time — touch targets must be solid, type must be readable under stadium lighting conditions (high contrast).
